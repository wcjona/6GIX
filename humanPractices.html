Human Practices

UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM

We wanted to better understand the extent of the impact and consequences of eutrophication in lakes and how to monitor water quality to detect early signs. While eutrophication is a problem that affects aquatic eco-systems worldwide we decided to focus on lake ecosystems locally in Alberta, thus we decided to reach out to the members at the Alberta Lake Management Society(ALMS). Specifically, we talked to Caleb Sinn, who is currently the program manager at ALMS and worked previously as a LakeWatch technician in 2019. One of the lake monitoring projects he mangages in the LakeKeepers project which involves monitoring lake conditions and water qualities and detecting early signs of eutrophication.






Caleb Sinn

Program Manager at Alberta Lake Management society

Ideate

We propose the use of 6gix, a water-soluble chlorophyll-binding protein in our chlorophyll detection system for determining water quality and indication of eutrophication in lakes.To verify the need of this solution we will speak to Rebecca Tasko, who is an Environmental Management Specialist with the Alberta Environment and Parks(AEP), as well as a director at the ALMS. She is also a professional biologist and she has worked as a surface water specialist doing environmental consulting for the past four years. We felt that her expertise would not only verify the feasibility of our solution from a scientific standpoint as well as its applicability and need for aquatic water management.

Design Solution



In order to correctly design and produce our chlorophyll-binding protein we will have to consult professors at the University of Calgary who have the right expertise. Approaching someone like Dr. Raymond Turner who is a biochemistry professor with significant experience in genetic construct design and recombinant protein production would be ideal.

Evaluate and Iterate on Design




Once we have produced and tested our 6gix protein we would like to host a faculty talk inviting all of our academic and industry contacts to evaluate the design of our solution and propose improvements. To further explore the application of our protein, we would like to reach out to Emily Hicks, who is the president and co-founder at FREDsense Technology to explore the protein’s potentials in the biosensor industry.
